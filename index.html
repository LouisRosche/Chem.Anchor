The Big Question: What happens when chemical bonds break and form? Where does the wax go when a candle burns?

üîç Evidence of Chemical Reactions

How do you KNOW a chemical reaction happened?

  • Color change - New substance = new color
  • Gas production - Bubbles = new gas forming
  • Temperature change - Gets hot or cold
  • Light production - Fire, glow sticks

‚öñÔ∏è Conservation of Mass

Atoms are REARRANGED, never created or destroyed!

Mass BEFORE = Mass AFTER

Example: 50g reactants ‚Üí Always 50g products (in closed system)

üî•‚ùÑÔ∏è Energy in Reactions

EXOTHERMIC: Releases heat ‚Üí Gets WARMER

  • Examples: Burning, hand warmers, rusting
  • Energy stored in bonds ‚Üí Released as heat

ENDOTHERMIC: Absorbs heat ‚Üí Gets COLDER

  • Examples: Instant cold packs, photosynthesis
  • Takes energy from surroundings

üîë Week 3 Key Takeaways

  • Chemical reactions create NEW substances by rearranging atoms
  • Evidence: Color change, gas bubbles, temperature change, light
  • Conservation of mass: Atoms rearrange but don't disappear (mass before = mass after)
  • Exothermic reactions RELEASE heat (burning, rusting, hand warmers)
  • Endothermic reactions ABSORB heat (cold packs, photosynthesis)
  • Reaction rates increase with: temperature, surface area, concentration, catalysts
  • Balance equations to show conservation of mass (same # atoms on both sides)

The Problem

2. Analyze data
3. Design device
4. Build & test
5. Iterate & improve

üîÑ Engineering Design Process

1

2

Choose reaction based on data. Sketch your device design. Consider: container, activation method, safety features, portability.

5

No design is perfect! What did you optimize? What did you sacrifice? Why is your design best for hurricane relief?
